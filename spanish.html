Spanish Tutorial

This tutorial repurposes material I wrote for a learning blog I kept during 2015-2016. In such blog, I reviewed materials for self-study of the Spanish language. I speak Spanish as a first language, and I used to be a Spanish tutor at that time. This particular post covers the learning website “Mi Vida Loca.” Mi Vida Loca is a funny, interesting and self-paced online program published by BBC in the UK; although, the program is currently archived.

Vocabulary

There are three vocabulary words that I’d like to review. They are all food related:

Vocabulary words:

  • Tostada
  • Merienda
  • Cafe con Leche

Before moving forward, I’d like you play the following short-video:

The table explains the meaning of the words.

Word in Spanish Description
Tostada It means “ toast.” Spain is the only place where I have lived where you can order a slice of bread in a coffee shop. You'll hear it used in episode 2.
Merienda It means afternoon snack. It is common to have something to eat either between breakfast and lunch, or in the afternoon.
Cafe con Leche It is a Coffee with milk. You will hear these words in episode 2 when the characters go to a restaurant.

Some grammar notes to consider include the following:

In Spanish, you will be using determined and undetermined articles. You can find a grammar lesson that reviews the articles here.

  1. Determined articles in Spanish are “el, la, los, las”. There is only one determined article in English and it is “the”.
  2. There is only one determined article in English and it is “the”.
  3. Undetermined articles in Spanish are “un, unos, una, unas.” The undetermined articles in English are “a, an”.
  4. The undetermined articles in English are “a, an”.
